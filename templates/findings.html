Key Findings

Exploratory data analysis and model evaluation on 918 patient records.

1. Dataset Overview

The dataset contains 918 patients — 508 with heart disease and 410 without. Males have a significantly higher rate of heart disease compared to females in this dataset.

2. Feature Correlations

MaxHR has the strongest negative correlation with heart disease (-0.40) — patients who can achieve higher heart rates during exercise are less likely to have disease. Oldpeak has the strongest positive correlation (+0.40) — higher ST depression during exercise signals poor cardiac function.

3. Chest Pain Type

Asymptomatic (ASY) patients have the highest heart disease rate. This is clinically significant — the most dangerous cases often present without obvious chest pain, which is why screening tools like this model are valuable.

4. Age Distribution

Heart disease patients skew older, with a concentration between 50-65 years. Healthy patients are more evenly distributed across age groups, with more representation in the 30-45 range.

5. Feature Importance

Random Forest identifies ST_Slope, MaxHR, and Oldpeak as the top predictors. These are all exercise-related measurements, confirming that stress test results are the strongest indicators of heart disease in this dataset.
